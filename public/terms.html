F1 Terms and Conditions

A pit stop is a crucial part of Formula 1 races where cars stop in the pit lane for refueling, tire changes, or repairs. Teams aim to complete pit stops as quickly as possible to minimize time lost during the race.

DRS (Drag Reduction System) is a mechanism that allows drivers to reduce aerodynamic drag on straights, increasing speed and enabling overtaking. It can only be used in designated zones during races.

Pole position refers to the first starting position on the grid, awarded to the driver who sets the fastest lap during the qualifying session. It provides a significant advantage at the start of the race.

The fastest lap is the quickest lap time set by a driver during a race. Drivers who achieve the fastest lap and finish in the top 10 are awarded an additional championship point.

Penalties are imposed on drivers for rule violations, such as exceeding track limits or causing collisions. Common penalties include time penalties, grid penalties, and drive-through penalties.

Formula 1 uses different types of tyres, including soft, medium, and hard compounds for dry conditions, and intermediate and wet tyres for rainy conditions. Tyre selection is crucial for race strategy.
